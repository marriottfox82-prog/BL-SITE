Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about Bandwolf membership, how features work, and what to expect as your band grows.

Membership & tiers

Bandwolf has two tiers: Free and Bandwolf Pro.

Free gives every member — including deps and front-of-house — full access to view setlists, RSVP to rehearsals, see band links, suggest songs, and add personal notes to songs. It's genuinely useful, not a stripped-down teaser.

Bandwolf Pro unlocks the tools that bands with serious admin needs use most: unlimited setlists, unlimited songs per setlist, Gig Mode for live performance, calendar export, and the ability to manage larger rosters and multiple bands. Pro is a per-person subscription — you pay for your own access, not for the whole band.

Deps, front-of-house & guests

Deps are a first-class use case. When a band admin invites you, you join as a regular member and get immediate access to everything you need for the gig: the setlist, song keys and BPM, any band notes, your own personal song notes, and the rehearsal schedule.

You don't need a Pro subscription to use Bandwolf as a dep. Free membership gives you everything a performer needs to prepare and turn up ready.

If you're depping across multiple bands, note that the Free tier supports joining up to 2 bands. If you regularly dep for more than two acts, Bandwolf Pro removes that limit.

Guests are a read-only role, useful for deps or crew who need setlist access but shouldn't be able to edit anything — even by accident. Guests can view all band content but cannot add songs, create setlists, or change any band data.

Regular members (both Free and Pro) can add their personal song notes, suggest songs, and RSVP. If they're on Pro, they can also create and manage content up to their tier limits.

Band admins assign roles when generating or sending an invite link.

Subscription & billing

Your account drops to Free tier. Everything you created while on Pro is still there — no data is deleted. The limits just snap back to Free levels:

  • You can still view all your setlists and songs, but won't be able to create new ones beyond the Free limits
  • Gig Mode and calendar export become unavailable until you resubscribe
  • If you were a band admin, you temporarily lose admin privileges. Your admin status is preserved in the background — it restores automatically the moment you resubscribe, with no action needed

Nothing is deleted. Resubscribing instantly restores full access, admin rights included.

Bandwolf subscriptions are managed through the App Store. You can cancel, pause, or change your plan in Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions on your iPhone, or directly at apps.apple.com/account/subscriptions.

You can also tap Manage subscription inside the Bandwolf app from your profile settings.

Cancelling takes effect at the end of your current billing period — you keep Pro access until then.

Bands & admin

Go to your band's Members tab and tap Invite. You'll get a unique invite code and a shareable link. Send either to the person you're inviting — they sign up (or log in) and join your band directly.

You can set whether they join as a regular member or a guest, and whether they get admin privileges, before you share the link.

Invite links can be set to expire or limited to a set number of uses, so you can safely share them in a group chat without worrying about unexpected joiners.

Features

Gig Mode turns Bandwolf into a live performance tool. It gives you a clean, distraction-free view of your setlist during a show — large text, dark background, tap to advance songs. It's designed to work in low-light environments and to be read at a glance from a music stand or monitor.

Gig Mode is a Pro-only feature. Any band member can view Gig Mode if the Pro member running the session shares their screen — they don't all need to be on Pro themselves.

Pro members can export their band's rehearsal and gig schedule as an ICS file, which can be added to Apple Calendar, Google Calendar, or any calendar app that supports the standard format.

You can export a single band's events or all your bands' events together. The calendar stays up to date — you can re-export any time to pull in new dates.

Adding a single rehearsal to your personal calendar from the rehearsal detail screen is free for all members — that's just a convenience link, not the full ICS export.

Find a time scans every band member's connected calendar and colour-codes each suggested slot by how clear it is. Green means everyone is free. Amber means there's a minor clash only — one or two members have a marginal calendar overlap, like an event that ends shortly after the slot starts; the slot is still worth considering and the affected members are listed underneath. Red means at least one member has a real conflict for most or all of the slot.

To stop tiny calendar entries from blocking otherwise free evenings, Bandwolf applies a 15-minute grace period at each end of every slot: an event that finishes within 15 minutes of a slot starting (or starts within 15 minutes of it ending) is treated as a minor clash rather than a hard conflict.

Members who haven't connected a calendar are assumed available — the checker can only see what it's been given access to.

Google Calendar, Apple iCloud Calendar, and any iCal/ICS feed — which covers Outlook, most work calendars, and most other calendar systems. Connect each calendar once in Settings and Bandwolf re-reads it automatically.

Bandwolf never reads or stores event titles, locations, descriptions, or attendees from your calendar. It only sees whether a time slot is marked busy or free.

Every song in a setlist has two layers of notes: band notes (visible to everyone, set by admins) and your own personal notes, which only you can see.

Personal notes are the place to put your own reminders: your capo position, a tricky transition you always forget, a cue from the singer. They travel with you across setlists and devices, and they're free for all members — no Pro subscription needed.
